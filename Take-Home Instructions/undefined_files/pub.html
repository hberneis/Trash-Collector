[Windows] Installation Instructions:

Connecting with serial communication:

  1.  Place your Jetson Nano on a non-conductive surface, such as the cardboard box that it came in.
    Important Warning

    Operating your Jetson Nano on conductive surfaces - like metals such as aluminum, copper, or silver - can short the pins on the bottom of the board causing damage to module components.
  2.  Use the microUSB data cable to connect the USB port on your computer to the microUSB port on your Nano module.
  1. Make sure that the green light in the corner of the Nano module is on.

  1. In the Device Manager, use the Ports (COM & LPT) section to find the COM number for your connection.
  1. If there are multiple COM devices in the list, unplug the data cable from the micro USB port on your Nano and make note of which COM devices remain listed, then reconnect the data cable and look for the new COM device added - this will identify the COM number for the serial line that goes to your Nano module!

 

  1.  Use PuTTY to open the serial communication line by setting the following:

  1. Category: Session
  2. Connection type: Serial
  3. Serial line: COM4
  4. Speed: 9600
    Once the session options are configured, feel free to save your current settings so you don't need to reconfigure for additional sessions moving forward. Then click the     Open button to begin your first serial communication session with your Jetson Nano module!

  1.  Enter the account login and password to access the terminal of your Jetson Nano module! As a security feature, your password will not appear on the screen as you type, but typing your password as normal and then pressing [enter] should still work as expected!

Connecting with SSH

  1. If you don't have the IP address for your Nano yet, follow instructions to set up your wifi connection below, then use the ifconfig command to find the inet IP address that you'll need to use SSH.
  2. Use PuTTY to open the ssh communication line by setting the following, where the IP address is the IP address for your Nano.

  1. Category: Session
  2. Connection type: SSH
  3. Host Name (or IP address): 192.168.0.231
    (This will change based on the IP address for your Nano)
  4. Port: 22

Once the session options are configured, feel free to save your current settings so you don't need to reconfigure for additional sessions moving forward. Then click the Open button to begin your ssh session with your Jetson Nano module!

  1.  Enter the account login and password to access the terminal of your Jetson Nano module! As a security feature, your password will not appear on the screen as you type, but typing your password as normal and then pressing [enter] should still work as expected!

[BOTH] Set Up Wifi Connection

  1. Locate the WiFi adapter in your nano kit. It is likely located in the box that the Jetson Nano comes in.
  2.  Plug the WiFi Adapter into any USB port on the Nano.
  3. Check whether your internet hardware shows up in the list of devices, you only need one of the two options: ethernet (eth0) through an ethernet cable or Wi-Fi (wlan0) through a Wi-Fi adapter.
  1. Ethernet will appear listed as connecting during initial IP configuration, then switch to connected after a few minutes once the internet connection is complete. Once connected, skip to step 4 to verify internet access.
  2.  Wi-Fi will appear as disconnected and you'll need to follow steps 2-3 to complete setup.
  1. nmcli device
  1.  Check that your Wi-Fi connection is showing up in the list of nearby networks. This will be the Network name that you set during internet sharing or the SSID of your home Wi-Fi network.
  1. nmcli device wifi
    Press [Q] to quit the list view and return to the terminal.
  1. Use the SSID listed in the previous step to replace [SSID] in the command below to request Wi-Fi access, enter your Nano password, then your Wi-Fi network password.
  1. sudo nmcli --ask device wifi connect '[SSID]'
  1.  Verify that you're getting internet access by pinging google and checking for 0% packet loss in the output.
  1. ping -c 3 google.com
  1.  Verify that your source is still connected, and check whether you're connected to the eth0 or wlan0 device.
  1. nmcli device
  1.  Run ifconfig combined with either eth0 or wlan0 depending on the previous output. Find the inet address and write it down to use for your SSH connection.
  1. ifconfig wlan0
  2. ifconfig eth0

Mac Installation Instructions:

Connecting with serial communication:

  1.  Place your Jetson Nano on a non-conductive surface, such as the cardboard box that it came in.
    Important Warning

    Operating your Jetson Nano on conductive surfaces - like metals such as aluminum, copper, or silver - can short the pins on the bottom of the board causing     damage to module components.
  2.  Use the microUSB data cable to connect the USB port on your computer to the microUSB port on your Nano module.
  1. Make sure that the green light in the corner of the Nano module is on.

  1. On your desktop, check for the mounted L4T-README drive. If you don't see the mounted drive, double-check that you're using the cable sent to you by iD Tech. The cable must be a data cable, so not every microUSB cable will work. Also, make sure you're plugging it in directly to a USB port on your computer, or that you're using a data-capable adapter.
  1.  Use a Spotlight Search to open a Terminal window, then run the command:
  1. ls /dev/cu.usbmodem*

The path that shows up in the results is the port that you should use for your serial communication connection!

  1.  Use the screen command to start a serial terminal session with the port you just identified:
  1. screen /dev/cu.usbmodem[Port-Number] 9600

  1.  Enter the account login and password to access the terminal of your Jetson Nano module! As a security feature, your password will not appear on the screen as you type, but typing your password as normal and then pressing [enter] should still work as expected!
    The preflashed microSD card is setup with account details:
  1. Nvidia login: nvidia
    Password: nvidia

Connecting with SSH

  1. If you don't have the IP address for your Nano yet, follow instructions to set up your wifi connection below, then use the ifconfig command to find the inet IP address that you'll need to use SSH.
  2.  Using the IP address for your Nano, run this command. For example, if the IP address on your Nano is 192.168.0.231, then the command would be ssh nvidia@192.168.0.321
  1. ssh nvidia@x.x.x.x

If prompted, type yes to add this connection to your known connections.

  1.  When prompted enter the password to your nano.
  2.  You are officially in your Nano!
